Greg is currently a Principal Ground Software Engineer for Rocket Lab. He completed his BS in nuclear engineering and MS in medical physics from the University of Wisconsin. After graduating, he took a job at Sandia National Laboratories in Albuquerque, NM where he worked on a launch accident safety team, analyzing the risk associated with rocket launches involving nuclear material. The job allowed him to travel to many places and continue his lifelong learning. The most memorable trip was to Cape Canaveral, Florida, where he was able to witness the launch of the Mars Science Laboratory, for which his team wrote the safety analysis report. He then returned to school to complete a PhD in aerospace engineering sciences from the University of Colorado. Following his PhD, he became a Mendenhall Postdoctoral Fellow in the USGS Geomagnetism Program where he worked on geoelectric hazards and wrote several software packages that are widely used by many researchers. After his postdoc he joined the Laboratory for Atmospheric and Space Physics (LASP) at the University of Colorado. At LASP he pioneered work blending research and software engineering, focusing on Space Weather applications. He was the Science Data Center Lead for NASA's IMAP mission, leading a team of ~10 software engineers in the implementation of algorithms for 10 different instruments from all over the world, and the infrastructure supporting delivering the data products to scientists and the general public. He also led the creation and development of models for the SWx TREC group (https://swx-trec.com) and bringing scientists' research code into operational environments and deploying to production systems.
